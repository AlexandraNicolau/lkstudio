Strength & grace

From a professional ballet dancer

Luba Kozharskaia

I was born in Saint-Petersburg, Russia. This is a city with great history and a world-famous culture. When I was 10-years-old, I decided to be a professional ballet dancer. From that year my ballet journey began. Eight years in the Vaganova Academy of Russian Ballet brought me to the famous Mariinsky theatre in 2005. I was happy to be a part of this company as it is the biggest ballet and opera theatre in Russia and Europe with amazing opportunities. I performed a wonderful and varying repertoire, worked with ballet celebrities such as Uliana Lopatkina, Diana Vishneva, Danila Korsuntsev, David Hallberg (American Ballet Theatre) and many other world-famous performers.

For about twelve years in this ballet company I have tried to absorb everything from ballet and related areas (history, styles, music, experience of people who were around me) and tried to apply it in my work.

In 2017 I have decided to finish my ballet career. I have realised that during the past 20 years of hard work and education I have gained unique experience in many aspects of fitness, choreography, injury management and rehabilitation. That is why I started studying in the Fitness College in Saint-Petersburg and became a Personal Trainer.

Through my education I recognised that I have valuable experience and knowledge to help people prevent different injuries and improve their overall well-being. I have moved to the United Kingdom in 2017 and since then I have been sharing my expertise and enthusiasm working as a ballet teacher, personal trainer and group class instructor. My work focuses on classical ballet choreography, personal well being, rehabilitation and pain management.
